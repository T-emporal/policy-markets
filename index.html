Policy Markets · Interactive AMM

A market where the traded instrument is a policy — a committed rebalancing program (φ, w, q). The AMM prices policies via a sinh-product invariant.
Definitions
policy (φ, w, q)
A committed rebalancing program. Not a directional bet — a rule. The trader commits to rebalance against deviations from their expected drift φ with reactivity w over q units of size.
φ drift angle
Trader's expected drift. Magnitude is conviction; sign indicates which way relative to consensus φ*. Range: (−∞, ∞), bounded to [−2, 2] in UI.
w sensitivity
w = 1 = trivial policy = just holding. w > 1 = trend-following (weight in asset moves more sharply than holding). w < 1 = contrarian (weight moves less, or opposite). Lives on a multiplicative scale: w=2 and w=½ are mirror-opposites in log space.
q size
Units committed. Linear amplitude of the policy's impact on the pool.
φ* consensus
Weighted mean of all committed drift angles — the current "midpoint" of the policy distribution. Not a fixed price; a moving reference frame that slides as commitments enter and exit. Simplified to 0 in this explorer.
Φ_L, Φ_S angular half-widths
The two "wings" of the committed policy distribution, measured upward (Φ_L) and downward (Φ_S) in drift-angle from consensus φ*. When the pool is balanced, Φ_L = Φ_S and the distribution is symmetric around φ*.
L, S wing weights
L = sinh(Φ_L), S = sinh(Φ_S). Not pots of tokens — cumulative commitment weight on each side of consensus, in sinh-angle coordinates. L grows as commitments enter above φ*; S grows as commitments enter below. The pool's net sensitivity is always 1 — the pool itself IS the asset (BTC).
K = L · S
Sinh-product invariant. Trades preserve K (slide along a hyperbola); liquidity events change K (jump between hyperbolas). Pool state is 2-dimensional: (K, tilt) or equivalently (L, S).
tilt = (L−S)/(L+S)
Asymmetry of the distribution around consensus. 0 = balanced. Positive = more commitment mass above φ*; negative = more below. Moves along the hyperbola when trades execute.
P = S / L marginal quote
The clearing price at the current pool state — what an infinitesimal next commitment would fill at. Moves when the pool tilts.
δ effective size
δ = q · log(w) · sinh(φ − φ*). Signed. Zero at w=1. Sign tells you which wing absorbs the commitment: δ > 0 pushes into L (pulls consensus upward), δ < 0 pushes into S (pulls consensus downward).
ρ sensitivity ratio
Dimensionless execution efficiency in (0, 1]. ρ = (value actually received) / (value at the clearing quote). A frictionless pool would return your commitment's full notional at the marginal price; the AMM returns ρ of it. ρ = 1 ⇔ no impact (infinite depth or infinitesimal trade). ρ → 0 ⇔ the pool was thin and most of your notional got eaten by moving the price against yourself. Not a one-time fee — it's the permanent efficiency locked into every future rebalance this policy makes.
slippage = 1 − ρ
Fraction of committed notional value lost to price impact. Identically equal to 1 − ρ — there is no independent notion of slippage here.
w_out = w_in^ρ
The counter-policy the trader receives. In log-space, log(w_out) = ρ · log(w_in) — ρ is the fraction of log-sensitivity preserved. Fixed point at w=1.
w_net = w_in^(1−ρ)
Net deviation from neutral the trader actually ends up with, after the AMM absorbs some.
deposit (BTC)
BTC numeraire extracted from the opposite wing when the pool absorbs your commitment. deposit = S·|δ|/(L+|δ|) for L-side commits, or L·|δ|/(S+|δ|) for S-side. Scales with |δ| and pool imbalance.
state manifold
The pool state lives on a 2D surface (L, S) ∈ ℝ²₊. Trades walk along constant-K hyperbolas (1 DoF); liquidity events move between hyperbolas (the 2nd DoF). Equivalent coordinate charts: (L,S), (Φ_L, Φ_S), (K, tilt), or (φ*, K).

Pool State · L · S = K

The pool state is the distribution of all committed policies, summarized in two numbers.

L and S are the wing weights — cumulative commitment mass on each side of consensus φ*, in sinh-angle coordinates. L counts policies committed above φ*; S counts policies committed below. They're not reserves of tokens; they're bookkeeping for how the committed population is distributed around the mean.

The curve L·S = K is the set of all states with the same depth. A trade (the pink preview) slides you along this hyperbola — shifting the balance between L and S, which tilts consensus toward whichever side absorbed the commitment. A liquidity event (not shown) would jump the entire curve inward or outward to a different K.

So the full state manifold is 2-dimensional: (K, tilt), or equivalently (L, S). Trades exercise the tilt axis; liquidity exercises the depth axis. Liquidity "stays centered around consensus" by construction: consensus is the midpoint of the distribution, not a fixed price.
