Policy Markets · Interactive AMM

A market where the traded instrument is a policy — a committed rebalancing program (φ, w, q). The AMM prices policies via a sinh-product invariant. The sensitivity ratio ρ is the permanent execution quality locked into every future rebalance.
Definitions
policy (φ, w, q)
A committed rebalancing program. Not a directional bet — a rule. The trader commits to rebalance against deviations from their expected drift φ with reactivity w over q units of size.
φ drift angle
Trader's expected drift. Magnitude is conviction; sign indicates which way relative to consensus φ*. Range: (−∞, ∞), bounded to [−2, 2] in UI.
w sensitivity
w = 1 = trivial policy = just holding. w > 1 = trend-following (weight in asset moves more sharply than holding). w < 1 = contrarian (weight moves less, or opposite). Lives on a multiplicative scale: w=2 and w=½ are mirror-opposites in log space.
q size
Units committed. Linear amplitude of the policy's impact on the pool.
φ* consensus
Weighted mean of all committed drift angles. The "market expectation" of drift. Simplified to 0 in this explorer.
L, S reserves
Two sides of the pool, with L = sinh(Φ_L), S = sinh(Φ_S). The pool's net sensitivity is always 1 — the pool itself IS the asset (BTC).
K = L · S
Invariant of the sinh-product AMM. Trades preserve K.
δ effective size
δ = q · log(w) · sinh(φ − φ*). Signed. Zero at w=1. Sign tells you which reserve absorbs the commitment: δ > 0 pushes into L, δ < 0 pushes into S.
ρ sensitivity ratio
Dimensionless execution efficiency in (0, 1]. ρ = (value actually received) / (value at the clearing quote). A frictionless pool would return your commitment's full notional at the marginal price; the AMM returns ρ of it. ρ = 1 ⇔ no impact (infinite depth or infinitesimal trade). ρ → 0 ⇔ the pool was thin and most of your notional got eaten by moving the price against yourself. Not a one-time fee — it's the permanent efficiency locked into every future rebalance this policy makes.
w_out = w_in^ρ
The counter-policy the trader receives. In log-space, log(w_out) = ρ · log(w_in) — ρ is the fraction of log-sensitivity preserved. Fixed point at w=1.
w_net = w_in^(1−ρ)
Net deviation from neutral the trader actually ends up with, after the AMM absorbs some.
cost (BTC)
BTC paid to execute the commitment. Scales with δ and pool imbalance.
D(δ) depth
Liquidity density at effective size δ. Falls exponentially away from consensus: D(δ) = e^(−α|δ|).
λ(δ) impact
Marginal price impact: λ(δ) = α / D(δ) = α · e^(α|δ|). Rises exponentially as commitments move away from consensus.

What is a Policy?

A policy is a committed rebalancing program: (φ, w, q) — drift angle, sensitivity, size. BTC itself is the trivial policy (φ*, 1, 1): consensus drift, unit sensitivity, unit size.

Depth Profile · D(δ) = e^(−α|δ|) · Impact λ(δ) = α / D(δ)

Liquidity is concentrated at consensus and falls off exponentially on either side.

The filled region (bids, green) and (asks, red) is the depth D(δ). Commitments near consensus (small |δ|) sit in the fat part of the curve — the pool can absorb them cheaply. Commitments far from consensus fall into the thin tails, where the same δ costs much more.

The dashed gold line is the impact function λ(δ) = α/D(δ). It's the reciprocal of depth: where liquidity is thin, impact is steep. The pink marker is your current commitment's position on this axis — if it's in the shallow tail, your ρ will be bad and your cost will be high.

Intuition: the AMM "believes" in consensus. It rewards traders whose expected drift aligns with the crowd (small |Δφ|) and penalizes traders with contrarian expectations, not by charging a fee, but by giving them worse execution.

Sensitivity Ratio Explorer · ρ = value received / value at clearing quote

ρ is a value-preservation efficiency, not a price. It's the fraction of your commitment's notional value that survives execution.

Imagine a frictionless pool with infinite depth: every unit of your commitment δ would execute exactly at the current marginal (clearing) quote, so you'd receive δ · P_clearing worth of output. The real AMM only returns ρ · (that ideal amount). The rest is eaten by the pool moving against you as you push into it. ρ is a pure dimensionless number in (0, 1] — no directional ambiguity, no "price of X in Y" to pick.

ρ = 1 ⇔ no impact (infinite depth, or infinitesimal commitment). ρ → 0 ⇔ the pool was thin enough that most of your notional value vanished into price impact — you "moved the price against yourself" so far that almost nothing came back.

Each curve shows how ρ decays as your drift gap |Δφ| grows, for a pool of depth K. Bigger pools (lighter curves) decay more slowly — same conviction, better fill. The highlighted curve is your current pool. The pink dot is your current commitment's (|Δφ|, ρ).

Why this matters: ρ is not a one-time cost. It's the permanent execution quality locked into every future rebalance that fires under this policy. A w>1 trend-follower with ρ=0.85 will, forever, translate 85% of log-sensitivity into actual exposure changes when the market deviates from their expected drift. Commit a policy with ρ=0.4, and every future rebalance leaks 60% of its intended action into the pool. Choose φ and q such that ρ stays high — or accept the erosion as the price of an unusual conviction.

The counter-policy you receive is w_out = w_in^ρ. At ρ=1 you get your full sensitivity back; at ρ=0 you get the trivial policy (w=1, just holding).
